### Question 533
**Question Type:** Single Choice  
**Topic:** Hardware Identification

A user asks a technician to install 16GB of RAM into their laptop. The user purchased DDR3-1600 RAM modules. The laptop currently has 4GB of DDR2-800 RAM installed. Which of the following should the technician do?

**Answer Choices:**
A. Install the DDR3-1600 modules and keep the existing DDR2-800 modules.
B. Remove the DDR2-800 modules and install the DDR3-1600 modules.
C. Install only the DDR3-1600 modules and dispose of the DDR2-800 modules.
D. Install the DDR3-1600 modules and keep one DDR2-800 module for compatibility.

**Correct Answer:** C

**Detailed Explanation:**
DDR2 and DDR3 are different generations of RAM with different physical connectors and electrical characteristics. They are not compatible with each other. The DDR2-800 modules must be removed completely before installing DDR3-1600 modules.

## Question 14 - Topic 1
**Question Type:** Multiple Choice

**Question Text:**  
A user submitted a support ticket that states all of the printouts from a laser printer appear to have double images imposed on them. A review of past printer support tickets shows that a maintenance kit has not been installed in more than a year. Which of the following printer consumables is MOST likely causing the issue?

**Answer Choices:**
A. Separation pad
B. Transfer roller
C. Ink cartridge
D. Fuser

**Correct Answer:** B - Transfer roller

**Detailed Explanation:**  
A double image (also called "ghost" or "repeating image") on laser printer output is most commonly caused by a worn or damaged transfer roller. The transfer roller transfers the toner from the imaging drum to the paper, and when it becomes worn, it can cause the image to repeat or appear twice.

## Question 18 - Topic 1
**Question Type:** Multiple Choice

**Question Text:**  
A user is trying to play a DVD on a projector. The user can hear the audio; however, the projector is showing an error message that states: "HDMI Blocked due to Copy Protection" Which of the following is the MOST likely cause of the error?

**Answer Choices:**
A. The HDMI cannot carry the signal from the DVD to the projector.
B. The user needs to switch from HDMI to a cable standard such as DisplayPort.
C. The projector does not support the necessary HDCP protocol.
D. The user needs to enable copy-protected sources in the projector's settings.

**Correct Answer:** C - The projector does not support the necessary HDCP protocol

HDCP (High-Bandwidth Digital Content Protection) is required for playback of protected content like DVDs. If the projector doesn't support HDCP, it will display this error message even though it can receive the signal.

## Question 19 - Topic 1
**Question Type:** Multiple Choice

**Question Text:**  
Vertical streaks are appearing on the output of a laser printer. Which of the following items is the MOST likely cause?

**Answer Choices:**
A. Roller
B. Drum
C. Transfer belt
D. Ribbon

**Correct Answer:** B - Drum

**Detailed Explanation:**  
Vertical streaks on laser printer output are most commonly caused by scratches or damage to the photoconductor drum. The drum is responsible for creating the electrostatic image that transfers toner to paper, and any damage to its surface will appear as streaks on printed output.
